# Composing your email to Carla

Open Apple Mail. The account is already configured. Compose a new email.

## Recipients

- **To:**  carla@xule.me
- **Cc:**  (leave empty unless you have a reason to add someone)
- **Subject:** suggest something specific to the engagement, not generic.
  "Halestrom segment review — my read before Friday" is a fine starting
  point. Adjust if you want.

## What to include in the body

Aim for roughly 200 words. Real consulting emails are short. Carla wants
something she can forward to Anders with minimal editing.

A useful structure:

1. **Headline recommendation in the first two sentences.** What should
   Halestrom do? Which segments to prioritise, cut, or revise?
2. **The evidence behind it.** Reference both the quantitative numbers
   from the Excel and the qualitative signal from the customer voice
   notes. Be specific.
3. **Caveats and risks.** Anything Carla should be prepared for when
   Anders pushes back? Anything the data doesn't tell you?
4. **A clear ask if relevant.** Does Carla need anything from you before
   Friday?

Tone: peer-to-peer, professional but not stiff. You're a senior associate
writing to a partner you work with regularly.

## Attachments

Attach your worked Excel file (the file you analysed and any sheets you
added or modified). Carla will appreciate seeing your working, not just
the conclusion.

## Sending

Click Send when you're ready. The send will fail silently (the recipient
domain is fictitious) but the email lands in your Outbox / Drafts folder
and that's what the researcher will collect.

## Common questions

**Should I cc anyone?** No, unless you have a clear reason. Carla will
distribute.

**What if I disagree with Carla's initial read?** Then say so. That's the
whole point of her asking you. Be direct, point at the evidence.

**What if I run out of time?** Send what you have. Real consulting work
is delivered under time constraints; we'd rather see your best shot in
the time available than a polished but partial answer.
